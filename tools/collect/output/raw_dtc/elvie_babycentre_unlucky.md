# Elvie pump- am I just unlucky?

[k](https://community.babycentre.co.uk/post/a33711460/elvie-pump-am-i-just-unlucky)

[Kerkkirsty](https://community.babycentre.co.uk/post/a33711460/elvie-pump-am-i-just-unlucky)

Posted 06-07-21

Hi mummas, Warning long post but stick with me! Jumped on from March group, I have an Elvie breast pump. I was sooo excited to buy what I thought was the best available. I thought I would be able to pop it in, pump and get on with day to day things. However a few days after I got it (they are £250 btw!) it leaked terribly. My bra and clothes would be soaked even if I was sat still. They eventually said I needed to buy a could steriliser as my steam one had ruined the parts (the instructions say steam is fine) Email advised to sit straight up right and still. Not what the ad’s show for sure! I was sent new parts then the device stopped charging all together. They sent a new pump. Now the pump I have appears to work (it’s making a noise like it’s pumping and the parts are moving) but no milk comes out. Once again they are asking me the same troubleshoot questions and refuse to give me my money back. I’m exhausted with it all. With my first baby I pumped large volumes with a second hand medela pump. I produce a lot of milk and can had express a few ounces quickly if boobs are full- I mean in moments just pushing my boob! Can’t get a drop out with this bloody pump. It’s making depressed and fearful our breastfeeding journey will soon end. With my first I had so much frozen I went back to work and didn’t have to use formula for months (no disrespect to formula!) I feel I can’t justify spending more money on a pump after ‘treating myself’ and ‘investing’ so much in this. Am I just really unlucky or have other had a bad experience with this machine? Thanks for getting this far
