[## Faulty product, Non-Existent support](https://www.trustpilot.com/reviews/68488ab457d363cf501b30bf)

I received a new Elvie double pump as a gift during my first pregnancy. After a miscarriage and a second healthy delivery, I was able to use the pump two years after it was initially gifted. I LOVED my pump at first and used it exclusively. After 3 months of usage however, one of the pumps would not turn on. There were no lights. Not responding to various charging cables and methods.

I reached out to customer service and found the experience frustrating. They sent me the troubleshooting steps which I had done, and then asked for a reciept to send a replacement pump. Because the initial gift was received two years ago, I don't have a gift reciept for it, and neither did the gift giver.

I did not want a replacement pump, I was just looking for assistance on getting my pump fixed. The rep informed me that "They follow strict policies" and "they don't have a repair department" and that my only options are to find a reciept or buy a new pump. Obviously I would not be buying a new product when I now have such a negative few of the current one, so the suggestion is entirely off-base.

Given that the unit costs $500+, I would have anticipated better solutions than "Turn it on and off again" and sending new part. I would have anticipated an RMA department, or local partnerships for repair support if the company is unable to stand up a whole department. I would also expect better recordkeeping/tracking of units to be able to trace back purchases.

One thing that was good is that it felt like there was a real human chatting on the other end rather than canned copy/paste or AI responses.

When the product works, its great. When it doesn't, it doesn't and there isn't much recourse. I will not be using Elvie products again.

June 10, 2025

[## Let down by poor processes](https://www.trustpilot.com/reviews/6826f4ecbc4168336a0c771e)
I’m writing this review to share my experience with others looking for a hands-free pumping option. I’ve been pumping since last October using the Elvie pumps, and compared to a more conventional electric pump it has allowed me the freedom to do things whilst pumping. I don’t use any other pump, so have used Elvie 4-6 x a day every day. The difficulty with these pumps (bought via Boots.com) is the short life on them and the customer service from Elvie:

-after six months I’ve come to realise the suction has reduced so have had to gradually increase the intensity. This is apparent by pressing play and watching the flange not moving as well as it used to, and at times, not moving at all (not fixed by substituting the flange)

-Elvie customer service are helpful to a degree in promptly replacing the hubs after a series of detailed questions over WhatsApp, the issue for me was the replacements are faulty as after only one or two uses the same issue is happening

-an additional issue is all of the replacements I was sent have faulty bottle release buttons so the bottle isn’t 100% attached to the hub and sticks when you release it causing some milk loss. I suspect this may be the issue some are having when they talk about them being leaky. You only know what you know, and I’ve only come to release this by comparing the buttons with my original hubs.

Out of the 3 replacements I’ve now been sent, only one has decent suction for part of the time and all have faulty bottle release buttons. when I’ve gone back to customer service to escalate all of this as a complaint there’s been complete radio silence, so my faulty replacement hubs aren’t being replaced, my messages are being ignored, and I’ve been told there is no complaints team. There is no one to write to on their website. This seems really poor!

In summary the hubs are great when they work, but won’t work for long. Beware of issues with the hubs and poor process by Elvie in dealing with these. If the warehouse had sent me hubs as good as my original ones from Boots to begin with my experience would be much more positive

[## Avoid the Elvie Breast Pumps, huge expense with poor results.](https://www.trustpilot.com/reviews/681d1d059cb303f6384dcb1b)
I had such high hopes for the Elvie breast pump especially as the price is so high, but i’ve had nothing but issues since purchase (9 months ago). 

The parts (valves and seal) stop working after a short period of time and then require costly replacements. The suction isn’t great on the pump and you can’t steam sterilise (had to get a separate bucket for milton) the pump is a faff to put together and then find it takes multiple attempts to get it working. I often get to the end of a pump session and find no milk (when breasts are full)- you cannot see if there is milk collected during the pump session due to the design of the machine. 

Customer service is a joke- they just send the same ‘how to’ guides you can find on website and always copy and paste the same (word for word) text about how they care about my experience etc etc- it is so disingenuous given that it’s an automated reply.

Lastly- the app is a joke- often tells me I’ve pumped 70-80mls despite the suction not working and only pumping a tiny amount.

I have started to use the Medela hands free pump and it is so much better. Better fit, better suction, better customer service, better ability to see how much milk you’ve pumped. 

I ignored poor reviews for Elvie and wasted my money on an overpriced product, I urge you not to do the same. Spend your money elsewhere and avoid the hassle.
