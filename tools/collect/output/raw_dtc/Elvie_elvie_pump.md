## Breast Pump Flange & Insert Size Guide

### Getting the right breast pump flange fit for your body is one of the most important parts of your pumping experience. If your pump doesn't fit you, using it could be like wearing the wrong bra. Awkward, frustrating, and pretty darn uncomfortable.

#### Do I need a single or a double pump?

Choosing a single pump means receiving one hub, allowing you to use on one breast at a time. Choosing double pump means receiving two hubs, this allows you to express milk from both breasts at once, saving time.

#### What are breast shields and nipple cushions and why might I need them?

Your pump uses a breast shield to connect your nipple with the pump. If it does not fit, it can affect milk volume and comfort. Elvie offers shields in 21 mm, 24 mm, and 28 mm.

If you have smaller nipples, you might need Elvie Nipple Cushions. Nipple Cushions are soft, silicone accessories that you can place inside a 24mm Breast Shield (which are included with all the breast pumps listed above) to help size down your shield and provide extra comfort. Elvie Nipple Cushions are available in size 15mm, 17mm, or 19mm. You can identify your fit with the table above.

To find the right combination for you, you need to measure your nipple. Follow the guidance below to learn how.

##### Why is breast pump sizing important?

No two ways about it. Getting the right size for you is kind of a big deal. It connects your pump to your body. If it doesn't fit, you might experience decreased milk volumes or even pain or discomfort. Not ideal, right?

##### What do I need to measure?

To get the recommended breast pump fit for you, you need to measure the base of your nipple. The base of your nipple is where it meets the areola. The areola is the circular patch of darker skin on your breast.

It’s important to size both of your nipples. No two nipples are the same, so you might find you need different sizes for left and right.

To avoid inaccurate measurements, do not measure yourself directly after pumping or breastfeeding.

![Image 1](https://cdn.shopify.com/s/files/1/0685/2322/9340/files/nipple-diameter.svg?v=1749747085)

*   Do not measure immediately after pumping or breastfeeding.
*   Measure at the base of your nipple, where it meets the areola (the darker skin area).

### How do I measure my nipple size?

![Image 2](https://cdn.shopify.com/s/files/1/0685/2322/9340/files/nipple-measurement.svg?v=1749747084)

1.   Grab a ruler and a debit/credit card.
2.   Perk up one of your nipples.
3.   Hold the card at the end of the ruler at a right angle (align card edge to 0 mm).
4.   In front of a mirror, place the base of your nipple in the ruler-card corner.
5.   The widest point at the base of your nipple is your size.

If something doesn’t feel right, there are a few steps you can take:
1. Familiarise yourself with Elvie Pump’s intensity settings.

Elvie Pump has 7 intensity levels for both Stimulation and Expression modes. When you press + to increase the intensity level, two things happen:

- The suction gets stronger

- The pumping cycles get slower

When you press – to decrease the intensity levels, the opposite happens.

- The suction gets softer

- The pumping cycles get faster

You can control the intensity settings for Stimulation and Expression modes separately. This means that when your Elvie Pump switches to Expression mode, you may need to adjust the intensity setting to suit your needs.
It may feel logical to use a setting which pumps faster, but if you are looking for a stronger suction, this will sound and feel slower. While higher suction works for some women, it’s not universal. For some, higher suction can reduce milk volumes and cause discomfort. It’s important to experiment and find the intensity settings that are right for you.

Top tip: when you reach the highest or lowest intensity setting, the lights on your Hub’s Play/Pause button will flash 3 times.

2. Check the personalisation settings in your app.

You can use the _Pump with Elvie_ app to personalise the intensity level that your Pump starts at in both Stimulation and Expression mode. The settings defined in your app are used:

- At the start of every new pumping session

- Regardless of whether you’re controlling a session from the app or offline by using the buttons on your pump.

- Regardless of whether you have changed intensity during a previous session.

It’s a good idea to check in with your app to see if your intensity preferences are up to date.
