The retailer forced me to deal directly with Elvie regarding warranty issues, as Elvie had instructed them to do so. However, Elvie claimed that only the hub has a 24-month warranty and that all other parts are covered for just 90 days, which directly contradicts the warranty granted by the retailer. Initially, Elvie instructed me to purchase the replacement part myself, even though the product was clearly still under warranty according to the sale agreement, which I was made with the retailer. In addition to the fact that I was required to buy a new spare part myself because the 90 days had passed, the problem was that the spare part was not available anywhere, and even Elvie itself could not provide any estimated restock date.

Later, when I started claiming my rights under the consumer protection law, Elvie agreed to replace the part under warranty but again stated it was out of stock with no delivery estimate. Since a breast pump is an essential product for feeding a baby, waiting weeks in uncertainty was not an option. The malfunction caused me health complications (including mastitis), and I was forced to urgently purchase an expensive replacement pump from another manufacturer.

The case only started moving forward after I informed Elvie that I would contact the consumer protection authority and explained the health impact caused by the faulty device. Only then did Elvie agree to send a full replacement device as a “gesture of goodwill.”

The entire process took several weeks, required dozens of emails, and demanded a strong understanding of consumer rights. Despite public responses suggesting smooth customer service, my real experience was the opposite: progress only happened after persistent pressure, legal references, and escalation.

My advice: 

– If you already own an Elvie product, know your consumer rights and insist on them firmly

– If you are considering buying this product, seriously reconsider whether you are prepared for a long and stressful warranty process

For a product as critical as a breast pump, reliability, spare part availability, and fast resolution are essential. In this case, they were not met.

***

Response to Elvie / For clarity to other readers:

I ultimately received a full replacement product from Elvie, not because of Elvie’s warranty policy, but because I invoked my statutory consumer rights.

I stated that I consider the product to have a type defect, as an essential component of this expensive device failed after only four months of use. Without a functioning connection kit (tubing), the pump completely loses its intended purpose and becomes unusable. Under consumer protection law in my country, the seller is responsible for providing a replacement or a refund when a defect cannot be resolved within a reasonable time.

It is also important to note that my purchase contract was made with the retailer, who granted a 24-month warranty for the entire product. I do not have a sales contract with Elvie, and therefore Elvie’s internal warranty policy (90 days for certain parts) cannot override the warranty granted by the seller.

The matter only progressed after I explicitly relied on consumer protection law and escalated the issue. This is relevant information for other customers facing similar situations.

***

I also went through the issue with the original seller and the consumer authority, and with the help of a consumer advisor I also received a monetary refund for the product, because the product defect could not be repaired within a reasonable time and the 24-month warranty was still valid.

It took a month to receive a full product refund from Elvie and two months to receive a monetary refund from the original seller, so I really recommend avoiding buying Elvie products.
