[## £159.99 Baby Monitor Failed Before 2 Years – Safety Concerns Dismissed](https://www.trustpilot.com/reviews/698671701f156c24d838c5fa)

£159.99 Baby Monitor Failed Before 2 Years – Safety Concerns Dismissed

I purchased the Dreamview Audio & HD Video Baby Monitor directly from Tommee Tippee in November 2023 for £159.99. At that price point, you would reasonably expect durability and reliability.

The monitor began failing before the two-year mark, but due to an extremely stressful period involving my young son’s ongoing medical treatment, I did not raise it immediately. I mistakenly believed the warranty expired in December rather than November and contacted Tommee Tippee only a few weeks after the warranty period had ended.

Within less than two years of use, multiple core safety functions had already deteriorated, including severe battery degradation (lasting only 10–15 minutes), delayed sound alerts (I could hear my child crying before the monitor alerted me), inaccurate temperature readings, and complete failure of the two-way talk feature.

This is a health and safety device relied upon for overnight monitoring. A baby monitor is not a cosmetic product — it is intended to support child safety. Core failures like these fundamentally undermine the purpose of the product.

Over the course of more than two weeks, I was passed between multiple members of the customer care team and management, including Florence, Becca (manager), Lanre, Morgan, Sam, Sophie and Susie. I provided detailed timelines, batch codes, troubleshooting responses, and raised specific questions regarding durability under the Consumer Rights Act 2015.

Despite this, the responses repeatedly reverted to warranty wording and selective references to the 30-day and six-month provisions of consumer legislation. The statutory durability requirement was never meaningfully addressed. The tone of the correspondence felt defensive and dismissive rather than solution-focused. Becca, as the manager, continued to restate warranty terms rather than engaging with the legal points raised, which was frustrating given the time and detail I had provided.

During the correspondence, Florence referenced that the company has a “good relationship with Trading Standards.” In the context of a consumer raising legitimate statutory concerns, this felt inappropriate rather than reassuring.

Ultimately, the final position was a refusal of repair or replacement and a 15% discount code towards purchasing another monitor.

If zero stars were an option, I would have selected that. I have only given one star because it is the lowest rating available.

This experience has significantly damaged my confidence in the brand. I will now be pursuing this matter via Citizens Advice for onward referral to Trading Standards and will not be purchasing Tommee Tippee products again. In future, I would choose to purchase through retailers such as John Lewis or Amazon, where I have previously experienced stronger customer service and more supportive aftercare.
