[## Warranty, what warranty?](https://www.trustpilot.com/reviews/69b1d78d5f89a1cac51658dd)

Warranty that only works if you have the receipt… even for a gift

I received a Momcozy V1 Pro breast pump as a gift and it failed after very little use. That’s when I discovered how their “warranty” works in practice.

Because it was a gift, I don’t have the original receipt. Despite the product having a serial/product ID that clearly identifies it, customer support refused to replace it without that receipt. For a product that’s very commonly bought as a gift, that requirement feels less like a reasonable policy and more like a convenient way to avoid honouring the warranty.

Their proposed solution was a voucher for their “official website.” The catch? The amount is listed in USD, even though I’m based in the UK, so it’s not even clear how it would work on the UK site. On top of that, the value is only about 10% off the RRP, meaning I’d still need to spend roughly £180 just to replace a product that failed shortly after purchase.

What makes it even more frustrating is the design of the pump itself. The motor unit can’t be purchased separately, and they don’t offer repairs, so even if the accessories and other parts are perfectly fine (which mine are, even got a second set of cups and all), you’re forced to replace the entire set if the motor fails.

That’s expensive, wasteful, and very convenient for the manufacturer.

When a product fails quickly, can’t be repaired, replacement parts aren’t sold, and the warranty hinges on a receipt the end user may never have had, it starts to feel less like bad luck and more like a business model, one that works very well for the company.

In hindsight, I wish I’d paid more attention to the negative reviews instead of the flood of glowing five-star ones.

March 10, 2026
